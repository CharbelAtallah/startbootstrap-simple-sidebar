World War II began in 1939 and ended in 1945

World War II can be seen as a logical continuation of the first World War - the "second act of tragedy" - because the series of events from the 1920s onwards that led to the outbreak of the second World War can be directly related to the peace that ended World War I. The war was fought between two camps. One side consisted of Germany, Italy and Japan. On the other hand, Britain, France, the United States and the Soviet Union cooperated. The war was a very great disaster, and it affected many more countries and people than any previous war.
